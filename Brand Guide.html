SECTION 1

Brand Overview & Story

Who we are, what the show is, and the identity that carries it.

Seasons Gone Bye is a 1990s Seattle urban fantasy: grunge-era streets above, ancient fairy courts below — well, above, technically, on floors 8 through 35. The brand must read instantly to an audience raised on Grimm, Buffy, The X-Files and American Gods, while staying unmistakably 1994 Seattle: rain, neon, tape hiss, and something older than the city underneath it all.

Wry & world-weary

A narrator who has seen too much and pours coffee anyway. Short declaratives, then a turn.

Mythic underneath

An earnest, old-magic floor beneath the wit. The city remembers; the brand should too.

Matter-of-fact uncanny

The supernatural is stated, never exclaimed. "The tide went out in 1889. Something stayed."

Forever 1994

Nothing in the visual or verbal identity that couldn't exist in 1994. No modern slang, no emoji, no app-store gloss.
THE ORGANIZING IDEA — ONE BRAND, THREE WORLDS

The show's premise (the seen and the unseen) is the design system's literal structure. One type system and two neons applied across three surface treatments: Night (pure black — podcast tiles, icons, social), Rain-slick twilight (indigo — the website), and Deep teal ink (green-black — posters and merch). Section 7 maps every medium to its world.

SECTION 2 · CONTINUED

Color Usage Rules

Restraint is the brand. Neon only means something against the dark.

✓ DO

  • Keep surfaces near-black; let one wash and one or two glowing elements carry the composition.
  • Use pink for brand moments and "the Unseen"; teal for wayfinding, UI states, and the world itself.
  • Code episodes and court content with the four seasonal hues — as dots, hairlines, and badges.
  • Reserve cream for short sacred lines ("THE COURTS KEEP THE DOOR") and schedule text.
  • Give type a dark halo before a glow when it sits over the tower mark or imagery.

✗ DON'T

  • Never more than two glowing elements per composition.
  • Never use cream (or any warm tone) as a surface — it reads bland and breaks the night.
  • Never introduce a third neon. Court golds/rusts/blues stay matte, not glowing, except their dots.
  • No pure white (#FFFFFF) text — use Text Hi or a neon halo tone.
  • No backdrop blur, no modern gradient meshes — washes and hairlines only.
RATIO GUIDE

Dark surface ≈ 85–90% of any composition. Neon (both hues combined) ≤ 10%. Cream ≤ 5%. When in doubt, remove one glowing thing.

SECTION 3

Typography

Five voices: engraved display, sign-shop labels, plain prose, an editorial whisper, and the tape deck.

Cinzel DecorativeDisplay — wordmark (700, glowing), section heads (400). Never below 18px. The courts' voice.
OSWALDUI & labels — always uppercase; 2.5px tracking for labels, 5px for eyebrows. The neon sign.
Libre FranklinBody — weights 300/400 for prose, 600+ sparingly. The plain-spoken narrator.
Cormorant GaramondEditorial — loglines, pull-quotes; italic carries the turn of a sentence. The old magic.
Courier PrimeMono — dates, runtimes, provenance, tape-box annotations. "SIDE A · RECORDED 03.94".

SECTION 4

Visual Motifs & Layout

Light behaving like light: glows, washes, hairline streaks, and one thin-line tower.

MotifSpecUse
Neon glowtext-shadow (type) / box-shadow (fills); pink, teal, or dual tokensWordmark, eyebrows, active states, buttons. Hover brightens the glow — never scales.
Atmospheric washOne radial/linear rgba gradient per surfaceclub (tiles), twilight (website), card (panels), teal-ink (posters)
Light streaks1px vertical gradient hairlines; pink west, teal east2–3 per composition max; rain-on-glass signature
Smith Tower markThin-line silhouette, pink→teal gradient stroke, no finial ballGhost @55% behind wordmarks; full glow standalone. Min height 48px.
Cards1px hairline border, radius 0–2px, corner washGlow border (pink) marks featured/selected only
Court dots9px circles, glowing, seasonal huesThe only round shape in the system

✗ THINGS TO AVOID

  • No rounded corners beyond 2px — this is sign-painting and stone, not an app store.
  • No drop shadows — glow is the only shadow.
  • No backdrop blur, no glassmorphism, no gradient-mesh backgrounds.
  • No daylight stock photography; imagery is night, rain, neon, wet asphalt, film grain.
  • No emoji, no exclamation points in display copy, nothing post-1994.
  • Motion: slow 400ms fades and glow transitions; flicker sparingly; nothing bounces.

SECTION 5

Voice & Tone

Wry noir over an earnest mythic floor — a narrator who has seen too much, in a city that remembers more.

SHORT DECLARATIVES, THEN A TURN

✓ WRITE THIS

"Four courts. One tower. Last call is at moonset."

✗ NOT THIS

"Discover the thrilling world of the four magical courts inside Seattle's historic Smith Tower!"
MYTHIC REGISTER EARNS ITS WEIGHT WITH MUNDANE DETAIL

✓ WRITE THIS

"The lobby smells of coffee and something older than rain."

✗ NOT THIS

"An ancient and mysterious power lurks beneath the city…"
THE UNCANNY IS STATED, NEVER EXCLAIMED

✓ WRITE THIS

"The tide went out in 1889. Something stayed."

✗ NOT THIS

"You won't BELIEVE what's hiding in Seattle's underground!"
ContextToneExample
Episode copyWry, atmospheric"The Autumn regent misses a payment, and the café's coffee starts coming out cold."
SocialTerse, sign-like"EP.07 DROPS AT MOONSET."
Website proseWarm noir, second person"Take a seat at the counter. One letter per moon; no glamour used on subscribers."
Live eventsGig-poster spare"A live reading · one night only · doors at dusk."

Naming & Terminology

The words of the world, used precisely.

TermUsage
Seasons Gone ByeThe show and the café. Always "Bye," never "By," in show contexts. Working title — see notice on page 02.
Blue Pine Media GroupThe producer. Appears in credits, poster header lines, and legal footers — never in the show's fictional world.
The four courtsCapitalized with season: "the Winter Court," "the Autumn regent." Collectively lowercase: "the courts."
The UnseenCapitalized. The hidden world and everything in it. Its color is neon pink.
The Boundary / the door"The Boundary" capitalized as the metaphysical border; "the door" lowercase, always singular. "The courts keep the door."
GlamourFae illusion, the old sense. Never "magic spell"; never the fashion sense.
MoonsetOne word, lowercase. The courts' closing time and the show's recurring hour.
EPISODE METADATA FORMAT
EP.07 · SIDE A · 03.94  "LAST CALL AT MOONSET"  43:12  WINTER COURT

Episode number with period; tape side; month.year date; title in quotes; runtime; court attribution. All in Courier Prime, as if typed on the tape box.

CREDITS LINE — USE VERBATIM

"Seasons Gone Bye is a fiction podcast produced by Blue Pine Media Group. Recorded in Seattle, on land the courts held first."

Application: The Three Worlds

Every medium belongs to a world. Don't mix worlds within one artifact.

MediumWorldRules
Podcast tile / app iconsNightPrimary tile: club wash, teal eyebrow, glowing wordmark, cream door-line. Export 3000×3000. At ≤64px the wordmark carries it.
Alt tile / avatarsNightGhost tower @55% behind haloed wordmark. Favicons: tower mark alone at full glow.
Episode artNightCourt-colored left streak + dot; mono metadata top; title ≤3 lines.
Social (square / story)NightOne glowing headline per canvas; court dot codes the episode; cream schedule line; no hashtags in the art.
WebsiteTwilightDusk surfaces, twilight wash, hairline streaks, ghost tower in hero. Teal = links/UI, pink = the one primary action per view.
Posters / merchTeal inkGreen-black ground, teal-ink wash, full-glow tower, pink only for the title moment. Mono event details over a hairline rule.
